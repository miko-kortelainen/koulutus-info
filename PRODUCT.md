# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Finnish university applicants (yhteishakijat), primarily 17–25 year olds making one of the most consequential decisions of their early adult lives. They arrive with a specific question ("how competitive is this program?", "which schools offer this degree?") and need a fast, low-friction answer. Some are doing this for the first time; others are reapplying and know the system. Both groups need clarity over depth.

## Product Purpose

yhteishaku.app lets applicants research Finnish university joint-application (yhteishaku) statistics: how many people applied to a program, how many start spots exist, and what degrees are on offer. The product turns raw Vipunen and Opintopolku data into a browsable, filterable interface. Success looks like: user finds the program they were thinking of, sees the applicant count and spots, and leaves with a clearer sense of their chances — in under 30 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, accessible, clear. The product doesn't lecture or overwhelm. It trusts the user to know what they're looking for and gets out of the way. Voice is warm but concise — Finnish directness without coldness.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard**: no glassmorphism, no hero metrics, no navy-and-teal "startup data" aesthetic, no gratuitous charts-for-charts'-sake.
- **Finnish government portal (Opintopolku.fi)**: not the heavy blue-and-white official portal aesthetic — yhteishaku.app should feel lighter and more approachable.

## Design Principles

1. **Disappear into the data.** The interface exists to surface numbers, not to impress. Every design decision should make the data easier to read, not the design more visible.
2. **Low friction from the first screen.** Students are already navigating a stressful process. No unnecessary steps, no decorative complexity.
3. **Consistent affordances.** Search, filter, and sort should feel the same on every page. One vocabulary, applied everywhere.
4. **Honest about uncertainty.** Where data is sparse (≤5 applicants shown as "~1–5"), the UI communicates this clearly rather than hiding it.
5. **Accessible to every applicant.** WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Color alone never conveys meaning. Works on a phone in a classroom.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 Level AA
- Dark theme is current default; should not compromise contrast ratios
- Finnish language UI — no localization needed beyond Finnish
- Reduced-motion support for any animations added later
- Touch-friendly on mobile (core use case for students on phones)
